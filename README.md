# Sample-Superstore-Sales-Analysis-Business-Performance-Insights
## Project Overview

Retail profitability is shaped by a complex interplay of geography, product mix, customer segments, and discount strategies. This project involves an **Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)** of the **Sample Superstore** dataset — a widely used retail dataset covering **thousands of US orders** across multiple years. The study focuses on uncovering what drives sales, erodes profit margins, and creates operational inefficiencies across product categories, regions, and customer types.

As a **Business and Data Analyst**, the objective is to transform raw transactional records into actionable intelligence that can help retail managers, category heads, and operations teams make smarter decisions around pricing, discounting, logistics, and regional strategy.

---

## Business Objectives

The analysis seeks to provide data-backed answers to the following critical questions:

1. **Profitability Analysis:** Which product categories, sub-categories, and individual products are driving losses vs. profit?
2. **Discount Impact:** How does the discount rate correlate with profit margin across different segments and categories?
3. **Regional Performance:** Which US regions and states are underperforming in terms of sales and profitability?
4. **Customer Segmentation:** How do the Consumer, Corporate, and Home Office segments differ in purchasing behavior and value?
5. **Shipping Efficiency:** What is the relationship between shipping mode, delivery lag, and order volume?
6. **Returns Analysis:** Which products and regions have the highest return rates, and how does this affect the bottom line?

---

## Dataset Description

The dataset is organized across **3 sheets** with **21 key attributes** in the primary Orders sheet:

| Feature | Description |
| --- | --- |
| `Order ID` | Unique identifier for each customer order |
| `Order Date` / `Ship Date` | Transaction and fulfillment dates |
| `Ship Mode` | Shipping method (First Class, Second Class, Standard Class, Same Day) |
| `Customer Name` / `Customer ID` | Customer identity and segmentation reference |
| `Segment` | Customer type: Consumer, Corporate, or Home Office |
| `Region` / `State` / `City` | Geographic hierarchy of the order location |
| `Category` | High-level product group (Furniture, Office Supplies, Technology) |
| `Sub-Category` | Granular product classification (e.g., Chairs, Binders, Phones) |
| `Product Name` | Full product description |
| `Sales` | Total revenue generated by the order line |
| `Quantity` | Number of units ordered |
| `Discount` | Discount rate applied (0 to 1 scale) |
| `Profit` | Net profit or loss on the order line |
| `Returned` *(Returns sheet)* | Flags orders that were returned ("Yes") |
| `Person` *(People sheet)* | Regional sales manager assigned to each region |

## Methodology & Technical Stack

### Analytical Workflow

1. **Data Cleaning:** Addressed missing postal codes, verified data types for date columns, and merged Returns and People sheets with the Orders table.
2. **Profitability Breakdown:** Calculated profit margin by category, sub-category, and state to identify loss-making segments.
3. **Discount-Profit Correlation:** Analyzed the effect of discount levels on profit to determine breakeven discount thresholds.
4. **Temporal Analysis:** Examined monthly and yearly sales/profit trends to identify seasonal patterns.
5. **Customer & Segment Profiling:** Compared order frequency, average order value, and profit contribution across the three customer segments.
6. **Returns Impact Assessment:** Joined the Returns sheet to quantify revenue and profit lost to returned orders.

---

## Key Insights (Executive Summary)

- **Category Risk:** **Furniture** — particularly the *Tables* sub-category — consistently generates negative profit, largely driven by heavy discounting, despite strong sales revenue.
- **Discount Trap:** Orders with discounts above **~20%** show a sharp decline in profitability across all categories, suggesting current discount policies are eroding margins.
- **Regional Disparity:** The **Central region** lags significantly behind East and West in profitability, with several states like Texas and Illinois showing net losses.
- **Technology Leads:** The **Technology** category delivers the highest profit margins, with *Copiers* and *Phones* being standout sub-categories.
- **Segment Value:** **Corporate** customers generate higher average order values, while **Consumer** customers make up the largest share of total orders.
- **Shipping Patterns:** **Standard Class** is the most used shipping mode, but **Same Day** and **First Class** orders correlate with higher-value customers in the Technology segment.
