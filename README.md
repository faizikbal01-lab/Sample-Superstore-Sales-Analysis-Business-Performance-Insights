# Sample-Superstore-Sales-Analysis-Business-Performance-Insights
Superstore Sales Performance Dashboard

An Excel-based business intelligence dashboard analyzing **$2.3M in sales** across 9,994 orders from a US retail superstore (2014–2017). Built as an assessment project to demonstrate data analysis, visualization, and reporting skills in Microsoft Excel.

---

##  Dashboard Overview

The workbook contains **3 structured sheets**:

| Sheet | Description |
|---|---|
|  Dashboard | Visual KPI summary with charts and trend analysis |
|  Summary Tables | Detailed breakdowns by category, region, segment, and month |
|  Orders Data | Raw dataset with 9,994 transaction records |

### Key KPIs (2014–2017 Cumulative)

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| Total Sales | $2,297,201 |
| Total Profit | $286,397 |
| Profit Margin | 12.5% |
| Total Orders | 5,009 |
| Unique Customers | 793 |

##  Key Findings

### Sales Growth
Revenue grew consistently from **$484K (2014)** to **$733K (2017)** — a **51% increase** over 4 years. Profit margin also improved from 10.2% to 12.7%.

### Category Performance
- **Technology** leads in profit ($145K, 17.4% margin)
- **Office Supplies** punches above its weight with strong margins
- **Furniture** is a concern — $742K in sales but only $18K profit (2.5% margin)

### Regional Insights
- **West** is the top-performing region ($725K sales, $108K profit)
- **Central** underperforms relative to sales volume ($501K sales, $40K profit — only 7.9% margin)

### Problem Areas
- **Tables** sub-category is loss-making: –$17,725 profit on $207K in sales
- **Bookcases** and **Supplies** also operate at a loss
- Heavy discounting appears to erode margins in several sub-categories

### Shipping
- **Standard Class** dominates at 5,968 orders (>59% of all shipments)
- **Same Day** is the least used at 543 orders

---

##  Recommendations

1. **Re-evaluate Furniture pricing** — especially Tables and Bookcases, which are loss-making sub-categories despite high sales volume. Consider adjusting discount policies or renegotiating supplier costs.

2. **Double down on Technology** — it generates the highest profit and margin. Investing more in this category (marketing, inventory, cross-sells) could significantly boost overall profitability.

3. **Investigate Central region performance** — Central has the weakest profit margin (7.9%) among all regions. A regional cost or discount audit is warranted.

4. **Leverage Q4 seasonality** — November ($352K) and December ($325K) are peak months. Stock planning and promotional budgets should reflect this pattern.

5. **Review discount strategy** — sub-categories with negative margins often have high discount rates. A data-driven discount threshold policy could recover significant profit.

6. **Focus customer retention on top states** — California ($458K) and New York ($311K) together account for over 33% of revenue. Loyalty programs here would have outsized impact.
